---
title: "Continuity"
tags:
- "MATH1051"
---

A [function](notes/MATH1051/functions.md) $f$ is *continuous* at a point $a \in \mathbb{R}$ if the [limit](notes/MATH1051/limits.md) of $f$ at $x = a$ exists and is equal to the function value at $a$, $f(a)$.

If $f$ is continuous at all values of $a \in \mathbb{R}$, $f$ is said to be continuous. If $f$ is not continuous at $a$, it is said to be *discontinuous*.
