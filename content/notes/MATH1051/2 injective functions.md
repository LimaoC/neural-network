---
title: "Injective Functions"
tags:
- "MATH1051"
---

A [[notes/MATH1051/2 functions|function]] $f: X \to Y$ is said to be *injective* or *one-to-one* if, for all $x_1, x_2 \in X$, we have $f(x_1) = f(x_2) \implies x_1 = x_2$.

Geometrically, a function is injective if it satisfies the horizontal line test (any constant horizontal line cuts through the function at most once).
