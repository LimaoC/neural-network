Let $f$ be a [continuous function](notes/MATH1051/continuity.md) on the closed interval $[a, b]$ and [differentiable](notes/MATH1051/derivative.md) on the open interval $(a, b)$. Then there exists a point $c$ in $(a, b)$ such that

$$f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}.$$

In other words, there is a point $c$ such that the tangent at $c$ is equal to the secant line between the endpoints of the interval $(a, f(a))$ and $(b, f(b))$.
