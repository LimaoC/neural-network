Let $f: X \to Y$ be a bijective ([injective function](notes/MATH1051/injective-functions.md) and surjective) function. The *inverse function* $f^{-1}: Y \to X$ is defined by $f^{-1}(y) = x$ for each $y \in Y$ and $x \in X$. We have $f^{-1}(y) = x \iff y = f(x)$.
