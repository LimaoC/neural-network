---
title: "Squeeze Theorem"
tags:
- "MATH1051"
---

Let $a_n, b_n, c_n$ be [[notes/MATH1051/3 sequences|sequences]] such that, for some $N \in \mathbb{N}$, we have $a_n \leq b_n \leq c_n$ for all $n \geq N$ and $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n = \displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} c_n = \mathscr{l}$. Then

$$\lim_{n\to\infty} b_n = \lim_{n\to\infty} a_n = \lim_{n\to\infty} b_n = \mathscr{l}.$$
